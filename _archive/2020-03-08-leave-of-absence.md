# Leave of Absence: March 8th, 2020

All,

Since I started on the Logistics UX project (consisting of teams Warloc & Brice) back in October, I have been thrust into a state of severe depression. I raised my issue with pair programming with my superiors, Alex Mathews and Felix Gilbert, before I started on the project. I have tried, and failed, to advocate for myself with my teams for solo development time. During retrospective meetings back when Pivotal was transitioning off of the project, I stated that I needed more time for "quiet contemplation" when working. This idea was dismissed due to full-time pair programming (aka - pairing) being the "de facto" standard for building software within Pivotal's culture.

I cannot stop ruminating about the traumatic events that have unfolded Thursday and Friday of this week. My entire weekend, I have struggled to reframe these incidents in a positive way, since they plague my conscious thought, and prevent the enjoyment of my time away from work. 

• During Thursday's retrospective (retro) meeting, Ethan Aquino lashed out at Kevin Wilson and myself. Ethan stated he does not approve of me working solo, and does not approve of me adding stories to our Icebox. I remained silent. I have exhausted my ability to vocally advocate for myself and my working style.

• Thursday evening, I attempted to explain via email (see below) how my disability prevents me from working in the "full-time pair programming" format which was established back when the LogUX teams were at Pivotal.

• During Friday's developer sync, with the dev team present, Ethan announced that he didn't want me working solo on certain parts of the codebase. I questioned him on this point, and asked if changes were done in a separate branch and adequately explained before merging (Merge Request Workflow, not the first time I've explained it to the team), would this change his stance? He replied in the negative. At this point, I asked to table this conversation, and requested our superiors be present for further discussion.

I'm going on month number five of being assigned to the Logistics UX project, with no hope for change in sight. Every retro, I try to advocate for myself. I have attempted, time and time again, to change the Warloc and Brice team's perception of how software development can be done, so that I might be an active contributor to our efforts. I have not succeeded. As a representative of Fearless, I feel that nothing I say is taken seriously. I believe that confidence in Fearless was destroyed the moment we declared we would not directly support the Warloc project.

This project has negatively impacted me psychologically, professionally, and financially. I have missed over a week of work in the month of February due to stress, sickness, and depression. I am absolutely dreading Monday morning. My mind is a wreck. I cannot focus. I do not know how to continue to slog through the massive dysfunction of this team and this project. 

This information should come as no surprise to my immediate superiors. I have been in regular communication with them regarding my morale. I am taking a leave of absence from work, starting Monday, March 8th. I apologize for the lack of notice. However, I trust that you will understand that I would not take such an extreme measure, unless it was absolutely necessary for me, and my health.

Sincerely,
Richard
