# How to Win Friends and Influence People - Dale Carnegie

## Chapter 1

* There is an unequal distribution of intelligence (really, opportunity).
* Criticism does not have the desired effect.
* Direct criticism of others does not yield correction of behavior.
  - it puts the person on the defensive.
* Don't expect others to be self-critical.
* Use the scientifically proven method of positive reinforcement.
* Don't criticize. Question.
* "Malice towards none."
* When confronted with strong feelings of criticism, write the letter (email).
  Don't send it.
* Admitting fault is necessary to learn and grow. The inability to admit fault
  means that an individual cannot learn. Admitting fault to something you have
  not done or are not responsible for is not advisable. Simply, own up to your
  actions.
* Mark Twain's wife would prevent her husband's letters of ill will from being
  sent by removing them from the mailbox.
* Improve yourself before trying to improve others.
* "I will speak ill of no man, [...] and speak all the good I know of everybody."
  - Ben Franklin
* It takes character and self-control to be understanding and forgiving.
* It takes strength to be gentle and kind. - Morrissey
* Instead of condemning people, let's try to understand them. - W. Livingston Larned


## Chapter 2: The Big Secret of Dealing with People

* The **only** way to get someone to do something is by making them want to do it.
* Threats and punishment only work temporarily.
* Everything humans do originates from two motives: sexual reproduction and the
  desire to be great (or important).
* People designate their importance by talking about their clothes, the car they
  drive, their children, their accomplishments.
* How you derive your feelings of importance determines your character.
* The "Dillinger Escape Plan" is to yell our your name: "I'm Dillinger!"
* "There are more patients suffering from mental illness in the United States
  than from all other diseases, combined."
* The ability to arouse enthusiasm in others is the greatest asset I possess.
  - Charles Schwab
* Once I did wrong and that I heard ever. For all I've done right, that I've heard never.
* Give praise to others, publicly and privately
* Problem: Runaway wives, Reason: lack of appreciation
* The power of appreciation.
* "There is nothing I need so much as nourishment for my self-esteem." - Alfred Lunt
* Encouragement is key
* Flattery is counterfeit and insincere. It is cheap praise. Appreciation is genuine.
* Appreciation is the legal tender that all souls enjoy.
* I shall pass this way but once; any good I can do or kindness I can show, let
  me do it now, for I shall not pass this way again.
* Every man I meet is my superior in some way in that I learn of him.
* Give honest and sincere appreciation to others.


## Chapter 3

* I prefer Strawberries and Cream. Fish prefer worms. Bait the hook to suit the fish.
* We are interested in what we want. To influence others, talk about what they
  want and show them how to get it.
* The whole purpose of advertising is to convince you that you want something.
  [ads shit in your head].
* The secret to success is the ability to understand someone else's point of view
  and to see their perspective, and compare it to your own. - Henry Ford
* When writing a cover letter, focus on the wants of the organization, and not
  your own.
* As a salesperson, show how your product can solve other's problems, and we'll buy.
* Think in terms of other people's point of view.
* Do not manipulate others. Both must gain from the interaction.
* Show others what they can achieve.
* How can I connect what I want to what they want?
* Self-expression is the dominant necessity of human nature.


## Fundamental Techniques in Handling People

* Don't criticize, condemn, or complain.
* Give honest and sincere appreciation.
* Arouse in the other person an eager want.
