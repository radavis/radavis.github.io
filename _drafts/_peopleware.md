# Peopleware

> Build one to throw away. It's only after you're finished that you know how the thing really should have been done.

Most managers of technical personnel fall victim to the same failing: viewing employees as modular, replacable components.

Somewhere between 15% and 25% of projects fail. Projects don't fail because of technical problems. They fail because of sociological issues; issues with human communication and interaction.

Punishing employees for making mistakes, pushing them to meet deadlines, thinking of workers as replacable components, evaluating workers by valuing quantity over quality, discouragement of thought and planning; these are the ways you can kill productivity and team morale.

Instead, as managers, we should encourage employees to learn from their mistakes, work to improve team dynamics, and recogize that each individual brings their own blend of experience and unique perspective to the team.

> People under time pressure don't work better; they just work faster.

> We all [thought workers] tend to tie our self-esteem strongly to the quality of the product we produce.

"The Flight from Excellence": allowing the market to decide the quality of the end result. A myopic view that disregards the professional's abilities. High quality and high productivity go hand-in-hand. That is if you want one, you need the other.

Parkinson's "Law": work expands to fill the time allocated. Parkinson's Law should not be applied to thought work. The authors pose a redefinition of this law:

> Organizational busy work tends to expand to fill the working day.

Thought work professionals are eager to complete any task assigned. However, they do not wish to sacrifice their self-imposed standards of quality.

On estimating work (time estimates): bad estimates are a demotivating factor. Productivity is highest when no estimate of work is defined, higher when an outside party (e.g. - "Systems Analyst") does the estimation.

Handling people effectively yields better team and project performance.

The pricipal work of software developers is human communication to organize the users' expressions of needs into formal procedure.

> The manager's function is to make it possible for people to work.

"Staying late or arriving early or staying home to work in peace is a daming indictment of the office environment."

"Coding War Games" and productivity non-factors. The following factors had little to no correlation to performance:

- Programming language
- Years of experience over 6 months
- Number of defects
- Salary

What does impact performance?

- Working environment
- Company culture
- Privacy
- Dedicated workspace
- Noise
- Interruptions

> Workers who reported [...] that their worplace was acceptably quite were one-third more likely to deliver zero-defect work.

You must measure productivity in order to improve.

> Your competition may be ten times more effective than you.
