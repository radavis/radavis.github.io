I cannot understand the complex without the ability to explain the subject
simply and succinctly. It is necessary to take the time to express the component
parts of a complex system in this manner in order to achieve a true
understanding of the whole, and, as a result, attain the ability to explain it
to others. This process takes time and energy. This thought process is work.

My mind has been trained to view work as resulting in a near-constant output.
Input raw materials, output finished product. In the realm of thought-work,
equation does not hold. Input does not yield output at the same rate, if the
input is ideas, concepts, and thoughts. I cannot simply "think harder" or
"think faster," to achieve more output. The correct environment may yield
quicker progress. The lower needs within the hierarchy must be satisfied for
work to occur. If I am hungry, or I have been deprived of sleep, I will not
work as efficiently as if my base needs have been provided for.

In many cases, it is necessary to say, "I do not understand." This can be a
major hit to the ego. Especially working in a field where you are expected to
know very much of the technical spectrum. You will need to say variations of
this phrase to multiple people in order to attain understading.

A ticket is not the responsibility of the individual, it is a responsibility of
the team. If a ticket is not clear, it is not actionable. Say, "I do not
understand," to enough people, until you have a clear path forward.
