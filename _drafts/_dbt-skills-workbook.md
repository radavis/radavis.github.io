# Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) Skills Workbook

DBT, deveoped by Marsha Linehan, is an effective method for helping others to
deal with overwhelming emotions and handling stress. DBT consists of four skills:

1. Distress tolerance: build up resiliency so that difficult circumstances are easier to handle.
2. Mindfulness: "living in the moment", instead of living in the past or the future.
3. Emotional regulation: observe your emotions, instead of becoming overwhelmed by emotion.
4. Interpersonal effectiveness: how to work better with others.

What are the ways I currently react to emotions that I want to change.

1. I become frustrated when presented with difficult work.
2. I get stressed out when my partner starts crying over money-related issues.
3. I lock up or lash out when the ideas that I present are challenged.

## Basic Distress Tolerance Skills

Common self-destructive coping strategies I use to cope with stressful situations:

- I spend a great deal of time thinking about past pains, mistakes, and problems.
- I get anxious worrying about possible future pains, mistakes, and problems.
- I isolate myself from other people to avoid distressing situations.
- I make myself feel numb with alcohol and drugs.
- I surrender to my pain and resign myself to living a miserable and unfulfulling life.

Possibe consequences of these coping strategies:

- You could miss good things that might be happening now, and regret missing those things.
- You could experience depression about the past.
- You might not ever overcome and learn from mistakes if you fret about them constantly.
- You could miss out on new and interesting experiences.
- You might spend more time alone, and as a result, feel more depressed.
- You could experience addiction, loss of money, work problems, legal problems, relationship problems, health consequences.
- You could get burned-out doing things for others.
- You might not get any of your own needs met.
- You could experience lack of enjoyment.
- You could suffer health problems from a lack of exercise.
- You might have regrets about your life.

These consequences result in pain and prolonged suffering.

We cannot control the pain in our lives, but we can control the amount of suffering we have in response. This is achieved through learning _distress tolerance skills_.

Ineffective coping strategies, which do not improve your situation:

- Emotional Pain leads to Anger/Agitation/Blame leads to Personal Suffering leads to More Emotional Pain.
- Constantly criticizing yourself -> You cannot change the past. You may only learn from it.
- Being overly judgemental
- "This situation should have never happened!" -> It did happen. Deal with it.

**Radical Acceptance**: acknowledge your present situation with no judgement. A very long chain of events that started far in the past have lead us here to this moment. Look at the present situation without judgement or blame in order to observe the facts of the matter. Radical acceptance is not condoning bad behavior, it's realizing you cannot change the past, and that being angry about the past or the situation only harms us. The correct response is to focus your attention on what you can do now to improve the situation.

Radical Acceptance Coping Statements:

- All the events have led to this moment.
- I cannot change what has already happened.
- Fighting the past only blinds me to the present.
- This moment is the culmination of millions of decisions.
- I cannot learn from this moment if I cannot let go of my anger.

Radical Acceptance Exercises:

- Read a controvesial story in the news without being judgemental.
- Wait in traffic without being critical of the situation.
- Review a past event and use radical acceptance to remember the moment without judgment.

Distract your thoughts. Forcing yourself to forget about a past event is impossible. Try to distract yourself with other memories or creative images.

- Remember pleasant moments from your past
- "Don't think of pink elephants."
- Keep a copy of your favorite quote(s) with you. Think of soothing imagery.
- Observe nature
- Imagine yourself as a superhero. What would you fix? What would people say?
- Imagine recieving praise from someone who matters to you. What would they say? Why does their opinion matter?
