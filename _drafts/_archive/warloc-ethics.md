As a self-described "War-hating bohemian Software Engineer" ramping up on the WARLOC project, I, too, have moral and ethical concerns. This application's current purpose is to streamline the documentation involved with maintenance of fighter jets. Currently, Air Force personnel can spend upwards of 2 hours on paperwork documenting the aircraft maintenance that occurred on their shift. WARLOC takes this pen-and-paper process to a digital format. When looking at this project through the lens of "process improvement," I can say that this project aligns with my personal goals and values as a Software Engineer: to make people's lives easier and to automate the mundane. When I look at this project from the perspective of "this app helps to prepare fighter jets for war and to ultimately take human life," this project does not align with my personal values.

Where do I draw the line when what I do helps others in the taking of human life? Whether or not aircraft maintenance and documentation is a pen-and-paper process or a digital one, it is a process that still needs to be done. - These are the thoughts I'm currently wrestling with.

For the moment, I'm choosing to view this project through the lens of process improvement. I'm choosing to focus the things I can do to ensure our organization is successful in this contract. However, I want us to continue to talk about this project and the moral and ethical concerns around it.
