The problem with many courses out there:

* Taught by technical people, too deep in their field. They don't understand,
or don't remember what it is like to be a beginner.
* Full of poorly-defined technical jargon. Authors attempt to speak to their
peers in the field, instead of tuning their language towards their audience.
* They assume knowledge.
* They fail to engage the reader with practical examples.


## Idea

Build an intermediate Java course.

* Resources for beginners
  - learn X in Y minutes
  - Java syntax overview
* VSCode setup, Atom setup
* Gradle
* lombok for getters and setters
* File I/O
* HTTP
* Streams
* Testing with JUnit
