Good and bad is relative to your chosen perspective.

You can choose to see the good or bad in any situation. If you choose focus on the bad, you can end up in a negative thought pattern, where you tend to focus on the things that are wrong, and then this effects your mood, negatively.

The converse of this is, if you look for the good in every situation, you can likely find some positive aspect of any situation.

Focus on the aspects of your situation that you can control, instead of the things you cannot, such as how you act in a given situation.


Good and bad is relative to your chosen perspective. For example, I might say to myself, "If I can finish this feature today, I will have a good day." Then, unforseen obstacles appear and instead of "having a good day and finishing the planned feature," I need to spend time refining my understanding of the technology I'm using before I can move forward. Or, I need to beef up my understanding of the problem domain before I can tackle the feature. Instead of calling it a "bad day," I can choose to shift my perspective and say, "I had a good day because I learned what I do and do not know about the problem I'm working on, and I now understand more about the problem than I did, yesterday."

A bad day at work is when I evaluate myself with a focus on the things that I cannot control.

A good day at work is when I remember to evaluate myself with regards to the things I can control.
