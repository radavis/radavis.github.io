# Open-office Etiquette

* Minimize distractions: Set your cellular phone to silent/vibrate. Turn off audio notifications for apps (e.g. - 'the Slack knock' and 'the Google Calendar chime' distract everyone within earshot).
* Be considerate of others: Use 'library voices' when talking in areas where others are 'heads-down' working. Look for indicators before interrupting someone. (e.g. - Someone with headphones on might be on a call, or might be blocking out conversations to achieve focus and might not want to be interrupted.) Check a coworker's calendar before tracking them down to ask a question. Ask them your question via Slack, or ask to schedule a time to talk in person.
* Utilize meeting rooms for meetings and phone booths for private phone conversations.
* Clean up after yourself: Utilize trash and recycling bins. Utilize provided cleaning supplies for small spills. For larger messes or facility issues, immediately notify a Spark staff member of the problem.
* Discuss and establish etiquette norms for your team room. Post them somewhere clearly visible in the room.
* Address etiquette problems politely. Everyone has a different definition of what qualifies as a distraction. Be polite when asking others to adjust their behavior. Listen to the concerns of others with an open mind.
* Take the time to create a "user manual," and make it available so others can learn about you and how to best work with you.
