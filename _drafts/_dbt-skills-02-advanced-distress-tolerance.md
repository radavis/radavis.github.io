**Radical Acceptance**

_dialectic_: balance and compare two different or contradictory things.

Within _dialectical behavior therapy_, you are balancing between change and acceptance.

- Change the behaviors that are not working, or that are creating more suffering.
- Accept yourself for the way that you are.

_Radical Acceptance_ is to accept something completely, without judgement. For example, accepting a present moment without getting angry or upset. The present moment has formed by a seemingly infinite chain of events that have lead up to this moment, events that have already taken place.

Recogize your role in the situation. Don't default to anger.

- What happened in this distressing situation?
- What past events lead up to this moment?
- What role did you play in creating this situation?
- What role did other people play?
- What do you have control of in this moment?
- What don't you have control of in this moment?
- What was your response?
- How did your repsponse affect your thoughts and feelings?
- How did your response affect others?
- How could the situation have occured differently if you had decided to radically accept the situation?

**Coping Strategies for Distressing Situations**

- Situation:
- Past Strategy:
- Unhealthy Consequence:
- New Strategy:
- Healthier Consequence:

- Situation: I'm faced with a bug or problem I can't figure out.
- Past Strategy: Get angry. Quit. Blame others.
- Unhealthy Consequence: Unemployment, loss of colleagues, loss of purpose.
- New Strategy: Don't blame. Assess what you know. Formulate a plan. Ask for help.
- Healthier Consequence: Keep employment. Keep colleagues. Maintain a sense of purpose.

- Situation: Someone corrects me, or critiques my opinion.
- Past Strategy: Get angry. Dwell on negative thoughts and emotions. Get frustrated that this other person doesn't see things my way.
- Unhealthy Consequence: Feeling sad. Move further into self-imposed isolation from others.
- New Strategy: Thank them for their input. Ask questions to further my understanding of their point of view. Accept the fact that we all see the world differently.
- Healthier Consequence: Feel good about having constructive dialog with others.

**My emergency plan for dealing with situations when I'm upset and dealing with other people.**

- First, I'll take a breath.
- Next, I'll short-circuit my anger by asking a question. Do you find that behavior effective? What makes you think that? How did you come to that conclusion? Tell me more.
- Then, I'll listen, without judgement.
- Finally, I'll accept that everyone sees the world through their own prisim shaped by their past experiences.

**My emergency plan for dealing with situations when I'm upset and alone.**

- First, I'll write down my thoughts and feelings.
- Next, I'll remind myself that I'm not perfect, and that I try my best, always.
- Then, I'll think backwards through the events that have lead up to this moment.
- Finally, I'll assess what I know, formulate a plan, and ask someone for help.
